Call for Papers

We invite researchers to submit work in any of the areas covered by the title, as well as the following: - Modeling of data that varies across space and time - Methods for deciding where to gather data - Bayesian optimization - Active learning - Probabilistic numerics - Probabilistic modeling of dynamical systems - Applications of the above to epidemiology, environmental sciences, socioeconomic sciences, engineering, and other areas A submission should be made in the form of an short 4-page paper using the [workshop's LaTeX style](/#) (available soon). Manuscripts should be anonymized in accordance with the [same rules as NeurIPS papers](https://neurips.cc/Conferences/2022/CallForPapers). References and appendices can extend as far beyond the 4-page limit as needed: we encourage use of BibLaTeX. If the submitted work has previously appeared in a journal or refereed workshop or conference proceedings, including the current NeurIPS proceedings to be published, the workshop submission should extend the previous work.

Submit your workshop paper by September 22nd anywhere on earth

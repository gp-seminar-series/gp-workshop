Call for Submissions

We invite researchers to submit work in any of the areas covered by the title, as well as the following: - Modeling of data that varies across space and time - Methods for deciding where to gather data - Bayesian optimization - Active learning - Probabilistic numerics - Probabilistic modeling of dynamical systems - Applications of the above to epidemiology, environmental sciences, socioeconomic sciences, engineering, and other areas A submission should be made in the form of a short 4-page extended abstract using the [workshop's LaTeX style](assets/gpsmdms_2022.sty). Manuscripts should be anonymized in accordance with the [same rules as NeurIPS papers](https://neurips.cc/Conferences/2022/CallForPapers). References and appendices can extend as far beyond the 4-page limit as needed: we encourage use of BibLaTeX. If the submitted work has previously appeared in a journal or refereed workshop or conference proceedings, including the current NeurIPS proceedings to be published, the workshop submission should extend the previous work. Code of conduct: participants must agree to the code of conduct for NeurIPS. Travel support: to encourage in-person participation for students with accepted papers who need financial aid for travel, we offer a limited number of travel awards. Please submit an application to be considered: https://forms.gle/aMCphghyfMjhDkUb6.

Submit your workshop paper by September 22nd anywhere on earth
